10 tips on how to get through university

Go to class

Try to attend every single class in university. It may be quite tempting to skip lectures due to the professors, but it is very easy to fall behind if one doesn't go to class.

Talk to people

Form study groups by talking to people in your tutorial section. This is strongly encouraged because the people you talk to in your tutorial section may be in your other classes.

Avoid buying food

Try not to eat out with your classmates or friends, as it is a waste of money. Instead, prepare meals and bring them to school with you.

Study on campus

Try to stay late and study on camous as much as possible. There is alot of distractions at home, which can prevent you from finishing assignments and preparing for tests.

Join clubs

When you are in university, try to join as many clubs as possible. This is a great way to make friends, discover your hobbies, and improve your resume.

Textbooks are not necessary

Don't buy textbooks unless a professor recommends one. They are expensive and sometimes not necessary.

Buy school supplies

Make sure you buy school supplies before the semester starts. No one is going to lend you their utensils in university, so buy

  • a board
  • markers
  • erasers
  • eraseable pencils
  • mechanical pencils and lead

Do not procrastinate

Try your best not to procrastinate. Procrastination can prevent you from finishing assignments and preparing for tests, which could hinder you from putting in your best effort.

Apply for scholarships

Try to apply for bursaries and scholarships. They immensely help with student loans and provide extra money on the side.

Find internships and research positions

Apply for internships and research positions. Experience is everything in the real world, so make sure you apply to every position possible
